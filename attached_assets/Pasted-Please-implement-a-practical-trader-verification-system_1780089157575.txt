Please implement a practical trader verification system in the MyLocalTrade app.

Important:
Do not rebuild existing trust badges, verified/unverified UI, or trader trust surfaces unnecessarily if they are already correct.
Reuse the current verification and trust UI where possible.

Goal:
Create a realistic trader verification flow that works for:
- business owners
- directors
- managers
- employees
- self-employed / sole traders
- authorised representatives of a business

The system must not assume that every trader is the legal owner of the business.

Main requirements:

1. Verification structure
Support separate verification layers:
- account verification
- contact verification
- business verification
- verified trader approval

2. Trader role in business
Add fields such as:
- owner
- director
- manager
- employee
- self-employed
- other

Also add:
- authorisedRepresentative: yes/no

3. Verification status flow
Add structured statuses such as:
- not_started
- pending
- needs_more_info
- verified
- rejected

Future-proof the model so it can later support:
- expired
- suspended
- revalidation_required

4. Business document support
Prepare support for verification documents such as:
- public liability insurance
- company registration proof
- VAT proof
- business address proof
- authorisation proof if the person is not the owner
- optional website or business email domain evidence

5. Sole trader path
Do not require a company registration number for sole traders or self-employed traders.
Allow a separate practical verification path for them.

6. Non-owner strategy
Allow non-owner traders to represent a business if they can show they are authorised.

Examples of acceptable non-owner proof:
- business email domain
- manager/owner confirmation
- business document linking them to the company
- insurance or other business documents showing the company details

7. Badge logic
Keep verified badge separate from:
- email verified
- phone verified
- business details submitted
- documents under review

Do not show full verified trader approval unless admin review is complete.

8. Public trust wording
Do not imply that MyLocalTrade guarantees a trader’s work or outcome.
Make the verified badge mean that business or representative details were reviewed according to platform checks.

9. Admin review
Add or improve admin review capability:
- review documents
- approve
- reject
- request more info
- leave internal notes
- record who verified
- record when verification happened
- record rejection reason or more-info reason

10. Verification audit fields
Add or prepare support for:
- verifiedAt
- verifiedByAdminId
- verificationNotes
- rejectionReason
- needsMoreInfoReason
- revalidationDueAt

11. Document safety
Treat verification documents as sensitive files.
Restrict access to admins only.
Do not expose uploaded documents publicly.
Do not show raw verification files on public trader profiles.

12. Safety
Do not break:
- auth
- trader profiles
- subscriptions
- dashboard
- enquiries
- existing trust UI
- reviews
- messaging
- admin flows

13. Output
After the audit and changes, summarise:
- what verification logic already existed
- what was added
- what remains placeholder
- whether non-owner authorised representatives are now supported properly
- whether sole traders are supported properly
- whether the verified badge wording is safe and not misleading
